About Me

From Ghana to Loughborough, Building AI That Matters

I am an AI Specialist and MSc Artificial Intelligence graduate from Loughborough University in the United Kingdom, originally from Ghana. I completed my BSc in Computer Engineering at the University of Energy and Natural Resources. I previously worked as an AI Research Assistant at Loughborough University on a project funded by the National Institute for Health Research (NIHR), which has now concluded. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, healthcare, and accessibility.

In my research, I have built multi-modal AI pipelines that integrate clinical imaging data and electronic records, improving retinal disease diagnostic accuracy by 15%. I have deployed a production melanoma classification system that processes over 1,000 medical image predictions every single day through a REST API. Earlier in my career, I developed an industrial IoT anomaly detection system that prevented over 12 critical infrastructure failures through unsupervised learning techniques.

"I built this course because AI felt intimidating when I started out, and I wanted to create the resource I wish had existed. Clear, honest, grounded in real applications, and free of unnecessary jargon."

My technical foundation covers Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, OpenCV, and Keras, with deep specialisations in computer vision, natural language processing, healthcare AI, and multi-modal learning. But the skill I am most proud of is this: the ability to take something genuinely complex and explain it in a way that actually makes sense to a human being.

I created this AI Crash Course to bring that same clarity to anyone who is curious about artificial intelligence. Whether you are a student, a professional exploring a career change, or someone who simply wants to understand the technology shaping our world, these 25 lessons are for you.
