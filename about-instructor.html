About Me

From Ghana to Loughborough, Building AI That Matters

I am an AI Specialist and MSc Artificial Intelligence graduate from Loughborough University in the United Kingdom, originally from Ghana. I completed my BSc in Computer Engineering at the University of Energy and Natural Resources. I previously worked as an AI Research Assistant at Loughborough University on a project funded by the National Institute for Health Research, which has now concluded.

In my research, I built multi-modal AI pipelines integrating clinical imaging data and electronic records, improving retinal disease diagnostic accuracy by 15%. I deployed a production melanoma classification system processing over 1,000 medical image predictions every day through a REST API. Earlier in my career, I developed an industrial IoT anomaly detection system that prevented over 12 critical infrastructure failures through unsupervised learning techniques.

My technical foundation covers Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, OpenCV, and Keras, with deep specialisations in computer vision, natural language processing, healthcare AI, and multi-modal learning. But the skill I am most proud of is the ability to take something genuinely complex and explain it in a way that actually makes sense.

I created this AI Crash Course to bring that same clarity to anyone who is curious about artificial intelligence. Whether you are a student, a professional exploring a new direction, or simply someone who wants to understand the technology shaping our world, these 25 lessons are built for you.

I built this course because AI felt intimidating when I started out, and I wanted to create the resource I wish had existed. Clear, honest, grounded in real applications.
